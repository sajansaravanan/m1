Messier 1: The Crab Nebula

Messier 1, also known as the Crab Nebula, is a supernova remnant resulting from a massive star's explosion observed in 1054 AD.

It contains a pulsar at its center, a rapidly rotating neutron star that emits beams of radiation.

This nebula is rich in elements such as hydrogen, helium, carbon, and oxygen, providing valuable insights into stellar evolution.

Located approximately 6,500 light-years from Earth, the Crab Nebula spans about 11 light-years across.

It is a popular target for astronomers due to its scientific significance and striking appearance.
